The Twisted Fate: Chapter 1

Sunday at 11:59 pm, in a small town in France, a stormy rain occurred strongly, as if it was crying for help. The sky was so dark with the lightning strike so loudly, which seemed to never end. At the moment, Mrs. Rosario luminè was giving birth to a daughter while the slave of their family name, Sophia, was also childbirth. As the rain stopped, Ms. Rosario saw her daughter getting out of the womb for the first time with the happiest smile on her face because her daughter was so beautiful and had very smooth fair glowing skin. The news of the mistress's daughter had made everyone in the mansion extremely joyful. The family even celebrated to welcome a new member to the family. According to their daughter's beauty, Master Luminè decided to name her “Albedo Luminè” due to stunning features like a flower shining brightly that no one could compare. On the other hand, Sophia gave birth to an unsightly daughter since there is a mark on her face. Yet, surprisingly Sophia looked so happy, false from everybody expected. Even though a huge mark was printed on the child's face, this kid's appearance deems soft and elegant in a unique way. Those reasons were supportive enough to give Sophia's daughter the name “Isabella”.

Years by years, Isabella turned 7 years old. One time, she was in the storage room all alone for one night, the reason for punishing was playing with the young mistress Albedo Luminè. Isabella sat down near one corner of the room, feeling grief for born without being loved by everyone. She rolled down her eyes, looked at everything in the room, touched the dirty on the floor, and started to cry in the darkness. Everything around her was like her lifestyle. The things that everyone looked over, hated, and throw away. She was in these situations. But suddenly, the hearing of stepping back of the shoes in front of the doorway made Isabella feel better, thinking that her mother came to look for her; however, she was wrong. Hearing head back of the shoes stepped, Isabella was misery. The overwhelming emotion tormented and insulted her. Isabella suffered every day when no one had seen her goodness, effort, how hard she tried. Instead of being complimented, she’d gotten a bad word. She imagined silent helping from her own mother, loved by everyone that she had never had, praised by people, and cheering for doing greatness.
